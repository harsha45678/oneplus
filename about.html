About Genesis


                 Dating back to 2005, Genesis is South India’s first and largest Inter Collegiate Academic Conference for Undergraduate medical students. One of the first colleges to host Inter-Collegiate quizzes in Tamilnadu Today Genesis’ flagship offline quiz is well reputed for being the longest medical quiz in India. For decades, case and poster presentations were traditionally held only for post-graduate students. Genesis was the first event to bring this experience to undergraduates in Tamilnadu.

                 Also Genesis is the first Medical Conference in Tamilnadu to conduct a Hack-a-thon event which encourages medical students to come up with innovative technological solutions to real-time medical problems Traditionally, the conference lasts for 4 days. The first two days offer students the opportunity to attend workshops on a vast array of clinical subjects conducted by some of the country’s most proficient clinicians. The next two days are dedicated to the flagship events to Genesis – the Junior and Senior medical quizzes.

                 The Genesis quizzes are well known for their marathon-like nature as they test participants’ mental fortitude and endurance like no other quiz. Conducted after a 3-year gap due to the COVID pandemic, come witness as over 2000 participants from across the country vie for the crown of the Champion of India’s Longest Medical Quiz.

About Madras Medical College


                 Madras Medical College is a crown jewel in the pantheon of distinguished medical colleges adorning the Indian landscape. As the 2nd oldest medical college in India, MMC boasts a rich and vibrant history spanning 177 years, and has been at the forefront of several medical advancements in the country.3 MMC has consistently churned out pioneers of excellence, such as Dr. Muthulakshmi Reddy, Dr. Guruswami Mudaliar, Dr. V. Shanta, Dr. Natesan Rangabashyam, Dr. Sarada Menon to name a few, and will continue to produce many more trailblazers in the field of medicine.

                 In the latest ranking released by CEOWorld Magazine, MMC stands 60th among the world’s top medical colleges, with only four other Indian Institutions ranking higher. In the latest rankings for medical institutions released by National Institutional Framework (NIRF), MMC stood 12th in the country.

                 Unknown to many, Madras Medical College has the unique distinction of having several of the country’s premier tertiary care hospitals attached to it. Rajiv Gandhi Government General Hospital (RGGGH, Park Town), The Institute of Child Health & Hospital (ICH, Egmore), Institute of Obstetrics and Gynaecology (IOG, Egmore), Regional Institute of Ophthalmology (RIO, Egmore), Institute of Mental Health (IMH, Kilpauk), Institute of Thoracic Medicine (Chetpet) to name a few.

About RGGGH


                 Founded In 1664 The Rajiv Gandhi Government General hospital (RGGGH), fondly referred to as The Government General Hospital (GH) is one such institution, which pioneered the cause of women's education, Not only is the RGGGH the first medical institution in the world to admit women, but it also holds the reputation for producing the first woman physician and gynecologist in the world, Mary Ann Dacomb Scharlieb. As being the land of first's (RGGGH) now has the State’s first stem cell/bone marrow transplantation unit in the government sector. Also, for the first time in Tamil Nadu, transgender people have access to an exclusive clinic at the Rajiv Gandhi Government General Hospital (RGGGH).

                 Within a few years of its founding, the hospital also went on to become a harbinger of countless achievements in the world of medicine, in a way shaping and revolutionizing the medical scene in India and the world at that time.

                 At present, the hospital has been a pioneer in making highly specialized healthcare services affordable for the masses, and is the epitome of success for the public health system. Having faced the brunt of COVID-19, the RGGGH has led the state's efforts to end the pandemic in Tamil Nadu, serving as a beacon of light in the past two years.
